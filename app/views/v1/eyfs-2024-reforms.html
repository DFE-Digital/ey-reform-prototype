2024 Changes to the early years foundation stage (EYFS) framework

The Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) statutory framework is mandatory for all early years settings. It sets the standards that all early years providers must meet to ensure that children learn and develop well and are kept healthy and safe.

The framework is for all Ofsted registered early years providers in all settings, including:

  • nurseries
  • childminders
  • pre-schools
  • reception

The framework will change on 4 January 2024 and you can find out more below about the changes and how they affect you.

Why the changes have been made

The changes have been made to ensure providers can operate successfully and practitioners can:

  • focus on providing children in their care with a high-quality early education
  • have opportunities to progress their careers

Changes for childminders

There will now be two versions of the EYFS: one for childminders, and one for group and school-based providers. Make sure you are referring to the right version for you when you need to.

  • check all level 2/3 staff members hold a valid PFA certificate, otherwise they should not be included in ratio

Childminders should know that:

  • settings will no longer be required to provide opportunities for children to develop and use their home language in play and learning â€“ consider what the best way to support children in your setting
  • you will no longer be required to complete training on the EYFS before registration
  • where judged appropriate by a childminder, childminding assistants can now fulfil the role of key person for a child
  • childminders with open plan kitchen-living areas can now utilise safely useable areas within this room for their provision
  • all level 2/3 staff members should hold a valid PFA certificate, otherwise they should not be included in ratio

Changes for group and school-based providers

There will now be two versions of the EYFS: one for childminders, and one for group and school-based providers. Make sure you are referring to the right version for you when you need to.

Group and school-based settings must:

  • ensure setting managers employed on or after 4 January 2024 must hold a level 2 maths qualification, or must achieve one within 2 years of starting in the position - make sure this is the case in your setting
  • ensure your settingâ€™s safeguarding policies and procedures include all electronic devices with imaging and sharing capabilities, not just mobile phones and cameras
  • check all level 2/3 staff members hold a valid PFA certificate, otherwise they should not be included in ratio

Group and school-based settings should know that:

  • level 3 practitioners in group and school-based settings will no longer be required to hold a level 2 maths qualification to count within staff:child ratios
  • a manager in group and school-based settings can now allow students and apprentices to count in staff:child ratios at the level below their level of study, if the manager is satisfied that they are competent and responsible

  • all level 2/3 staff members should hold a valid PFA certificate, otherwise they should not be included in ratio
